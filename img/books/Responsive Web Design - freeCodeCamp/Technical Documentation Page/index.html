Product life span

The average lifespan for a domestic cat is 15 years. Spayed and neutered cats live much longer than cats who are unaltered.

Home installation

Preparing your home

It is very important to make preparations before your new cat enters your home. Some cats may be less demanding than other. However, most new cats will expect some basic requirements to be met upon entering your home for the first time.

Installation requirements

  • The home must be a clean environment
  • Do not worry to tell your new cat which rooms belong to them. Your new cat will claim their rooms without your assistance. Warning: be prepared to find out that your room now belongs yo your new cat.
  • There must be a designated area for your new cat's food bowls. Most cats will allow you to choose this space.

Recommended accessories

  • Cat tree
  • An empty box
  • Catnip

Holding your cat

Be patient. Your cat will make it known when they wish for you to carry them in your arms.

Maintenance

Fuel requirements

  • Water

Interior maintenance

Your cat should have access to food and water at all times. It is best to keep a steady supply of fuel accessible.

Exterior maintenance

Your cat has self-grooming protocols already installed. There is no reason to ever bath your cat. Doing so could result in an angry cat. You do not want to see your cat when they are angry.

Troubleshooting

Your cat is working exactly as intended. Defective cats do not exist.
